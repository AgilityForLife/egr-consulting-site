PMP Eligibility Requirements: Do You Qualify?

Before you start studying, you need to confirm you meet the PMI eligibility requirements. The good news: if you have project management experience, you likely do.

The Two Pathways

PMI recognizes two educational pathways for PMP certification:

  • Bachelor's Degree or Higher: You need 36 months (3 years) of project management experience within the last 8 years, with at least 35 hours of project management education.
  • High School Diploma or Associate's Degree: You need 60 months (5 years) of project management experience within the last 8 years, with at least 35 hours of project management education.

The 35 hours of formal project management education can come from university courses, online training, certifications, or instructor-led workshops. This is why many candidates pursue PMP training near me options—to check both the knowledge and experience boxes efficiently.

Experience Calculation

Your project management experience counts if you've been directly involved in planning, executing, monitoring, controlling, and closing projects. This includes:

  • Leading cross-functional teams
  • Managing budgets and timelines
  • Risk management and stakeholder communication
  • Quality assurance and project documentation

Pro Tip: Start documenting your project management experience now. When you apply through PMI's portal, you'll need to describe specific projects, your role, dates, and hours. Having this information organized saves time and ensures accuracy.

What to Expect on the PMP Exam: Format and Structure

Understanding the exam format reduces test anxiety and helps you prepare more effectively. The PMP exam has been significantly redesigned, and it's important to know what you're walking into.

Exam Overview

Aspect Details
Format 180 multiple-choice and multi-select questions
Time Allowed 230 minutes (3 hours 50 minutes)
Passing Score Minimum of 135 correct answers (approximately 75%)
Cost $555 for PMI members, $705 for non-members
Delivery Computer-based at testing centers or online proctored

Question Types

The exam tests your understanding of the PMBOK framework through scenario-based questions. You won't see simple definition questions. Instead, you'll get situational scenarios where you must choose the best action a project manager should take. This requires both knowledge of frameworks and judgment gained from experience.

Questions span five process groups (Initiating, Planning, Executing, Monitoring & Controlling, Closing) and ten knowledge areas (Integration, Scope, Schedule, Cost, Quality, Resource, Communications, Risk, Procurement, and Stakeholder Management).
