For Individuals: Your Action Plan

Immediate (This Month): Audit your current capabilities honestly. Do you have working knowledge of AI applications in project delivery? Can you articulate the difference between Agile and traditional PM, and when to use each? Do you have modern PM certification, or are you relying on older credentials? This audit isn't about shame; it's about clarity on where to focus.

Near-Term (Next 3 Months): Start building AI fluency. This doesn't require specialized training necessarily. Take free courses on AI applications in business, experiment with AI tools in your daily PM work, read case studies of how organizations are integrating AI into delivery. The goal is to move from "I've heard of AI in PM" to "I can make informed decisions about where AI adds value." EGR's "AI for Project Managers" course condenses this into a focused learning experience.

Mid-Term (Months 4-6): If you don't have PMP certification, pursue it nowâbefore July when the exam changes. The new exam is more rigorous and better aligned with modern delivery, but the preparation window is tight. If you already have PMP and it's current, great. If it's expired or expiring, plan your recertification around the new content.

Longer-Term (Months 6-12): Pursue advanced Agile credentials. SAFe Program Consultant (SPC) is the gold standard, but it requires hands-on experience. If you're not ready for SPC, pursue SAFe Practitioner or Release Train Engineer. These credentials are increasingly required for senior PM roles, and they're in short supply. Organizations will invest in your development if you're committed.

Career Positioning: Start talking about your development goals openly with your manager and HR. If you're working toward AI fluency, Agile scaling expertise, and modern PM certification, frame it as strategic investment. Organizations that understand talent market dynamics will support your development. Those that don't will lose you to competitors who will.

"The time to prepare for the future is before the future arrives. By then, the advantage is already captured by those who moved first."

For Organizations: Your Transformation Path

Assess Your PM Capability: What percentage of your PM population has modern skillsâAI fluency, Agile experience, contemporary certification? Be honest. Most enterprises find that their core PM population is stronger in traditional approaches than in modern delivery. This is an opportunity to invest, not a crisis.

Invest in Training and Certification: Organizations that treat PM development as a cost center struggle to retain talent and deliver transformation. Those that treat it as strategic investment see dramatically better outcomes. Budget for focused training in AI applications, Agile scaling (SAFe), and modern PM certification. The ROI is significantâbetter delivery, faster innovation, lower turnover.

Design Hybrid Delivery Models: Don't force everything into Agile or everything into traditional PM. Design delivery models that choose the right approach for each program. Some projects thrive with full Agile. Others benefit from a hybrid model that combines predictive planning with adaptive execution. PMs who understand both and can architect the right model are invaluable. Train them to do this.

Embed AI into Project Governance: AI isn't a future-state thing. Implement it now. Start with low-risk applicationsâpredictive risk detection, automated reporting, intelligent resource recommendations. Get your PM population experienced with AI tools. As they use AI daily, they'll develop the fluency organizations need. Organizations that wait until AI is fully mature will be behind.

Align Your PMO for Modern Delivery: Your PMO should be helping programs deliver value, not enforcing compliance theater. Evaluate your governance, reporting, and approval processes. Are they enabling modern delivery or constraining it? PMs want to work in organizations with intelligent governance. Build that, and you'll attract and retain the talent market is competing for.

2-3 years
is the typical timeline for an organization to shift PM capability from traditional to modern. Start now if you want to be ready for 2028 market dynamics.

The Talent Gap Is Accelerating

Here's the uncomfortable truth: demand for modern PM talent far exceeds supply. Organizations worldwide are investing in AI integration, Agile transformation, and digital delivery. They all need experienced PMs who understand these domains. The supply of people with deep expertise across AI, Agile, and governance is small. The supply is growing, but slowly. For the next 2-3 years, talent scarcity will be acute.

This creates opportunity for individuals willing to invest in development. If you build the trifecta, you'll have genuine career options. If you wait until 2028 to worry about these skills, you'll be competing with thousands of others in an oversaturated market.

For organizations, this means you must invest in developing your internal talent now. External hiring for highly skilled PMs is expensive and competitive. Developing your existing population is more cost-effective and often more successfulâyou retain institutional knowledge and build culture.
